History

2018–2020: Formation through Star Hunt Academy

Laurenti Dyogi, ABS-CBN's Head of Entertainment Production, launched the Star Hunt Academy (SHA) program in 2018 to nurture young Filipino talents. From 250 auditionees, the first set of female trainees was selected and eventually trimmed down to eight members. These trainees, including former Pinoy Big Brother housemates Sheena and Gwen, were trained by local and South Korean coaches from 2018 to 2020. The group debuted as SHA trainees in August 2019, performing at various events, including the PBB Otso Big Night and the 2019 Manila Southeast Asian Games thanksgiving celebration. In 2020, they showcased their progress through performance videos and participated in the Philippine K-pop Convention's Happy Hallyu Day 4 event.

2020: Pre-debut activities, "Da Coconut Nut"

Bini's pre-debut single was an electropop remake of Ryan Cayabyab's "Da Coconut Nut," produced in collaboration with MU Doctor Academy and South Korean music production group Vo3e. Choreographers Moon Yeon-joo and Kwak Seong-chan, who had worked with popular K-pop groups, created the choreography. The official lyric video for "Da Coconut Nut" premiered on Bini's YouTube channel on November 6, 2020, followed by the music video on November 20. Bini performed "Da Coconut Nut" live for the first time on It's Showtime on November 21. On December 4, Bini signed contracts with Star Magic and Star Music.

2021–2022: Debut with "Born to Win"

On May 4, 2021, Bini released their first official debut teaser, leading to the announcement of their debut single "Born to Win," which was released on June 4. Their debut launch, "Bini: The Runway," featured a fashion and music event showcasing creations by designer Francis Libiran, followed by "The Showcase" on June 11, where they performed and interacted with fans. Bini performed live on ASAP and participated in the Philippine K-pop Convention's Happy Hallyu Day 5 event in August. They released their third single, "Kapit Lang," on September 10, and performed a modified version of "Born to Win" at the Miss Universe Philippines 2021 pageant. In February 2022, Bini was featured on the cover of Dubai-based magazine Xpedition, and on September 29, they released their sophomore studio album, "Feel Good."

2023–present: Domestic success, "Pantropiko" craze and Talaarawan

In late 2023, Bini released "Karera" (Race) and "Pantropiko" (Tropical) as pre-release singles to promote their first extended play (EP) Talaarawan (Diary). "Karera" became popular in school graduation ceremonies due to its resonant themes, while "Pantropiko" topped the Spotify Charts’ Daily Top Songs for the Philippines in April 2024, becoming a summer anthem. The release of Talaarawan on March 8, 2024, coincided with International Women's Day, aligning with their advocacy for women's empowerment. The success of "Pantropiko" was amplified by its viral TikTok dance challenge, leading to over two million monthly listeners on Spotify by the end of March, making Bini the highest-streamed female OPM artist and the most-streamed P-pop group in the country.

In 2024, Bini announced their first major solo concert, "BINIverse," at the New Frontier Theater, celebrating their third anniversary. Tickets sold out quickly, prompting additional dates and calls for a larger venue, with all dates eventually selling out. The concert’s third day was announced to be streamed on iWantTFC. Bini also guested on the Chinese reality show Show It All and announced their first North American tour, with stops in Vancouver, Edmonton, Winnipeg, and Toronto. In June, they entered the Top Artists Global chart on Spotify, launched their website, and announced a new single, "Cherry on Top," set for release in July. They celebrated their third anniversary with "National Happy BINI Day" and performed at Philippine independence festivities, although the performance was cut short due to safety concerns. Bini topped Spotify's Daily Top Artists Philippines chart, surpassing Taylor Swift, and held a successful marathon event titled the “BINI Run” in Manila.
